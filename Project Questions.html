1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

INDEPENDENT - Word Condition (Congruent or Incongruent)
DEPENDENT - Response Time in seconds

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

Ho - Null Hypothesis: ( i - c = 0 ) There is no significant difference in the averge response time in viewing the congruent(c) words vs viewing the incongruent(i) words. Ha - Alternative Hypothesis: ( i - c 0 ) There is a significant difference, positive or negative, in the average response times.

The Dependent Samples t-Test is the appropriate statistical test as the same subjects are assigned two different conditions. In addition, we don't have any population parameters (so a z-test would not be appropriate here).

Conclusion:¶

We FAIL to reject the Null Hypothesis which states that there is no significant differences between the average completion time for the two different conditions (Congruent & Incongruent). In theory, if we took a random person from the population we wouldn't expect them to be significantly slower or significantly faster in either condition. These are not the results I expected, but I realize that the results were close to being significant at the 90% level (1.711-1.6487 = 0.0623) and if took another sample of 24 subjects we may confirm my anecdotal experience that there is a significant difference. This has taught me that in some cases there may be a practical significance or a perceived difference and it still might not be considered statistically significant.
